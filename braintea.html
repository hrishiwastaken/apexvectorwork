01 — About

Why we opened a practice that pours slowly.

I trained in hospitals where care was measured in fifty-minute slots and “progress.” It worked for the paperwork. It rarely worked for the person in the chair.

The Brain Tea started as a simple idea: what if getting help felt like being handed a warm cup — a place to slow down, be believed, and think out loud without being rushed toward a fix? We kept the clinical rigour and threw out the clinical coldness.

Today we're a small team of psychologists, counsellors and a collaborating psychiatrist. We see people online and in our Bengaluru studio, and we take the time each person actually needs — no scripts, no upsell, no waiting-room fluorescent hum.

steep, don't scramble.

The name

“Brain Tea” — a warm cup for a whirring head.

Tea only works if it's given time to steep. So does the mind. The name is a small promise: we won't hurry the parts of you that need to settle before they can change. Come in tense; leave a little more yourself.

Mission

To make genuinely good mental-health care feel warm, unhurried and human — for everyone who walks in, whatever they can bring.

Vision

A world where asking for help feels as ordinary, and as gentle, as making yourself a cup of tea.

Safety first

Nothing important happens in a body that's bracing. We build the ground before we dig.

Plain honesty

We'll tell you what we think, what we can help with, and when someone else is the better fit.

Your pace

You set the speed. Some weeks we go deep; some weeks we just make the room feel safe.

Human, not corporate

Real people you can actually reach — by phone or WhatsApp — not a ticket number.

No surprises

Clear fees, clear formats, clear next steps. You always know what you're booking.

Quality care

Licensed, experienced clinicians who keep learning — and are supervised, not winging it.

What we believe
You can't reason your way out of a body that thinks it's in danger. So we start there.
— The Brain Tea, on why we go slow

07 — Good to know

Questions people ask first.

Do you offer online sessions?

Yes — nearly everything we do is available online across India, and in person at our Bengaluru studio. You choose the format when you book, and you can switch between them.

How do I reschedule or cancel?

Just message the clinic. A real person handles all changes by phone or WhatsApp so nothing gets lost in a form. We ask for 24 hours' notice where possible.

What does a first session cost?

Fees depend on the clinician and range from ₹1,400 to ₹2,000. Every therapist's fee is listed on their profile, and you'll see the exact amount before you pay. Payments are via UPI or card through Razorpay.

I'm not sure what I need — where do I start?

Start with Individual Counselling and choose “no preference” for the therapist. We'll match you, and if someone else on the team is a better fit, we'll say so and move you over.

Is what I share confidential?

Yes. Sessions are private and held to professional ethical standards. We only break confidentiality in the rare situations the law requires (serious risk of harm), and we'd talk you through it first.
