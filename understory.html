The person who started it

I got tired of watching people get rushed.

In my first job, sessions were fifty minutes and the goal was progress. But you can't reason your way out of a body that's convinced it's in danger.

So Understory does it differently. We slow down until slowing down stops feeling scary — and we build from there. No pushing you toward the thing you're not ready to touch. No treating a panic response like a scheduling problem.

Fourteen years in, I still think the most useful thing I do is help someone's shoulders drop half an inch. Everything real tends to follow that.

What actually happens here

Three things we do that most rooms don't.

We go slowly, on purpose.

Some weeks we don't get anywhere near the hard thing. That isn't a wasted session — it's your system learning it's allowed to rest in here.

We pay attention to your body, not just your thoughts.

When your chest tightens or your jaw sets, we say so out loud. That signal is information — and it's usually sitting right on top of the real work.

We regulate before we excavate.

Nobody gets asked to relive their worst day before they've got ground under their feet. Steady first, always. The digging can wait until it's safe to dig.
